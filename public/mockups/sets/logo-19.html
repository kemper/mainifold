Twisted vase · helix
A part, caught mid-make — and reaching. The primary mark is a tapered, square-section twisted column that prints itself from the build-plate up: teal slabs deposit one by one and stack, and because each slab is rotated a little more than the last, a candy-twist emerges as it rises — four corner-edges spiral up in lit teal against the darker turning faces, a soft teal print-head glow riding the live layer. It tops out into the finished tornado form, holds, then drops back to the plate and runs again (~4.6 s). The resting frame is the completed column, so any still looks polished; favicon, monochrome and on-light hold a bold spiral silhouette legible down to 16 px.
