Impossible interlock
Two chunky parts — a teal block ring and an amber block ring — interlocked like chain links, except the weave is impossible: each square tube threads cleanly through the other, so the amber part is at once in front of and behind its mate. Every ring is a solid square-section tube with a lit top face and a shaded side wall, so it reads as real machined volume — an assembly the renderer almost built but that can't exist. That ties the impossible-geometry idea straight to a maker of parts. The two-link silhouette stays unmistakable down to a 16 px favicon, and never reads as a play button or arrow. On light: dark navy + amber parts.
